1. Assignment overview 

This document covers the project plan, task ownership, tooling, and timeline for the Group 5 Big Data Management assignment. There are two equally weighted deliverables: 

 

    Assignment 1 (50%): A 3,000-word report covering data quality control, model development and analytics, and interpretation of results. 

    Assignment 2 (50%): A 15-minute video presentation covering background of methods, steps performed, implementation detail, pros and cons, and interpretation of results. 

 

The dataset is the Istanbul Stock Exchange (ISE) dataset. The target variable is ise2 (USD-based ISE returns). The seven input variables are daily returns from: S&P 500 (sp), DAX (dax), FTSE 100 (ftse), Nikkei (nikkei), Bovespa (bovespa), MSCI Europe (eu), and MSCI Emerging Markets (em). 

 

The in-person presentation takes place on 29 April 2026. Both online submissions are due by 7 May 2026 at 12pm. 

2. Marking criteria 

    Criteria 1 (10%): Presentation quality, structure, referencing, and clarity. Lucid, well-written, logically structured. All sources correctly referenced in Harvard style. 

    Criteria 2 (50%): Thorough, comprehensive, and accurate analysis. This is the dominant criterion. Every technical section must be exhaustive, precise, and argued through a sustained, coherent, and convincing level of analysis. 

    Criteria 3 (20%): Use of relevant academic literature and course material. Distinction level requires excellent depth of both classic and contemporary reading. Minimum 10 high-quality academic sources. Add sources as each section is drafted - not at the end. 

    Criteria 4 (20%): Critical approach, clarity of recommendations, and implications of findings. Every section must include critical evaluation. Do not just describe - assess, compare, and justify every methodological choice.

4. Tooling and software 

4.1 Coding environment 

Both programmers use VS Code with the Jupyter extension. This mirrors industry-standard data science and fintech practice. Notebooks (.ipynb) are used for exploratory and output-generating work; reusable functions are refactored into .py scripts where appropriate. All code is version-controlled via GitHub. 

 

4.2 Version control 

A private GitHub repository is created and shared between the two programmers from day one. Non-coders do not require access. Commit messages must be descriptive and reference the relevant section (e.g. "S1: add IQR outlier detection with visualisation"). A clean commit history demonstrates professional practice and provides a safety net against overwriting. 

 

4.3 Report writing 

All writing is done in Microsoft Word via OneDrive with track changes enabled. One master document is maintained. Each member owns their designated section. The final formatting pass consistent headings, spacing, font, and page layout is performed by Jasneek before submission. 

 

4.4 Referencing 

Harvard referencing is managed throughout the project using Zotero, which integrates directly with Word via a browser extension. References must be added as each section is written. A full reference audit is conducted before the final submission date. 

 

4.5 Video production 

Members record their segments using Loom (browser-based, no setup) or OBS (free, higher quality). Raw MP4 files are sent to Jasneek by 26 April. Jasneek produces the final video in Premiere Pro: cuts, colour grade, lower-thirds titles, transitions, and audio levels normalised. The completed video is exported and submitted before the 29 April presentation.

7. Report structure (3,000 words) 

Suggested word allocation to meet the 3,000-word requirement at distinction level: 

 

    Introduction (~200 words): Dataset context, global index relationships, research framing. Brief mention of ISE significance as an emerging market proxy. 

    Section 1 - Data quality control (~600 words): Dataset dimensions and feature types. Missing value identification and chosen imputation method with justification. Outlier detection method (IQR, Z-score, or similar) with treatment rationale. Distribution analysis for each variable with visualisations. Summary statistics table. Written by Vansh. 

    Section 2 - Model development and analytics (~1,200 words): Univariate vs multivariate decision with justification. Supervised vs unsupervised choice with reasoning. ML methods selected, compared, and critically evaluated. Feature importance or variable contribution analysis (addressing the brief's variable contribution question - treat as feature importance for ISE prediction). Correlation analysis between inputs and target. Model evaluation metrics and comparison. Advantages and disadvantages of each approach. Written by Vansh (methods) and Sahar (justification and critical evaluation). 

    Section 3 - Interpretation (~600 words): Results interpreted for a finance practitioner audience. How findings could inform investment or portfolio decisions relating to ISE exposure. Implications and limitations. Written by Suaad. 

    Conclusions (~200 words): Summary of key findings, recommendations, and future work directions. Written by Sahar. 

    References: Harvard format, minimum 10 academic sources. Managed by Vansh via Zotero. 

 

Important clarification: The assignment brief contains a reference to identifying variables that contribute to 'churn.' This is a copy-paste error from a different dataset. Interpret this as identifying which global indices are the strongest predictors of ISE returns. This should be noted briefly within Section 2 to demonstrate critical reading of the brief.

The video must address: background of methods used, explanation of steps performed, how they were implemented, pros and cons of the model, and interpretation of results. Suggested allocation per member: 

 

    Introduction + dataset background (~2 min): Sahar - global index context, ISE relevance, research question framing.

Recording instructions: Use Loom or OBS. Include a face-cam window alongside the screen share. Speak at a measured pace - aim for clarity over speed. Slides must be visible throughout each segment. Send completed MP4 files to Jasneek by 26 April. Jasneek will handle all editing, titles, transitions, and audio normalisation in Premiere Pro. 

9. Targeting distinction - key requirements 

Distinction (70%+) at MSc level requires work that goes beyond accurate analysis. The following are non-negotiable for the highest grade: 

 

    Sustained critical argument: Every methodological choice must be justified with reference to literature and the specific characteristics of the dataset. Do not just apply a method - argue for it. 

    Depth of literature: Both classic and contemporary academic sources required. Do not rely solely on textbooks. Include recent peer-reviewed papers on stock market prediction with ML. Minimum 10 sources for distinction. 

    Model comparison: Do not run a single model. Compare at least two approaches (e.g. linear regression as baseline vs a tree-based or neural method). Evaluate using multiple metrics (RMSE, MAE, R-squared, or equivalent). Discuss why one outperforms the other in this context. 

    Regression vs classification decision: Clarify with Dr Mandal. If ambiguous, present both framings - predict the return value (regression) and predict direction up/down (classification). This demonstrates analytical depth and earns marks under Criteria 2 and 4. 

    Business relevance in Section 3: Finance members should frame results in terms of portfolio management, emerging market exposure, or hedging decisions. Generic interpretation will not reach distinction. The audience is a finance practitioner, not a computer scientist. 

    Video production quality: A professionally edited video with clear titles, consistent audio, and well-structured flow reflects on Criteria 1 for Assignment 2. Treat it as a client-facing deliverable. 

    Harvard referencing: Every factual or methodological claim must be cited. Missing or incorrect references directly reduce the Criteria 1 and 3 scores. 

10. Key risks and mitigations 

 

    Code-to-report dependency: The 18 April code freeze is mandatory. Writing cannot be finalised without final plots and model outputs. Programmers must communicate any delays immediately. 

    Regression vs classification ambiguity: Raise with Dr Mandal at the next lecture. If unresolved, present both framings in the report - this strengthens the analysis. 

    Brief copy-paste error (churn language): Treat as feature importance for ISE prediction. Include a brief note in Section 2 to demonstrate critical reading. 

    Referencing left until the end: Vansh must add Zotero entries throughout the writing phase. Catching up on references at the last minute reduces quality and risks missed citations. 

    Video recording quality: All members should test their recording setup before 22 April. Check microphone levels and lighting. Poor audio quality in raw footage limits what can be recovered in editing. 

    Uneven contribution: All five members have clearly defined deliverables. If any member is unable to complete their task, the group must communicate early enough for others to cover it without affecting the submission timeline.
